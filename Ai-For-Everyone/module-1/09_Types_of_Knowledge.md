# Introduction

Artificial Intelligence requires different kinds of knowledge to solve
different kinds of problems.

Some knowledge stores facts, some explains procedures, some is based on
experience, some describes relationships, and some provides information
about existing knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Types of Knowledge

The major types of knowledge are:

1.  Declarative Knowledge
2.  Procedural Knowledge
3.  Heuristic Knowledge
4.  Meta Knowledge
5.  Structural Knowledge

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Declarative Knowledge (Know What)

## Definition

Declarative Knowledge represents facts, concepts, and information.

Examples:

-   Delhi is the capital of India.
-   Water boils at 100°C.
-   Earth revolves around the Sun.

Applications:

-   Search Engines
-   Digital Encyclopedias
-   Knowledge Graphs

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Procedural Knowledge (Know How)

## Definition

Procedural Knowledge describes how to perform a task or process.

Example:

``` text
Start Car
   │
Press Clutch
   │
Change Gear
   │
Drive
```

Applications:

-   Robotics
-   Industrial Automation
-   Navigation Systems

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Heuristic Knowledge (Experience-Based)

## Definition

Heuristic Knowledge is based on experience, practical rules, and
shortcuts that help solve problems efficiently.

Examples:

-   Doctor's experience in diagnosis
-   Chess AI selecting promising moves
-   Troubleshooting techniques

Applications:

-   Expert Systems
-   Medical AI
-   Game Playing AI

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Meta Knowledge

## Definition

Meta Knowledge is knowledge about existing knowledge.

It includes:

-   Source
-   Reliability
-   Confidence
-   Scope

Example:

``` text
Prediction Accuracy = 97%
```

Applications:

-   Expert Systems
-   Medical Diagnosis
-   AI Assistants

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Structural Knowledge

## Definition

Structural Knowledge describes relationships among concepts and how
knowledge is organized.

Example:

``` text
Animal
 │
Mammal
 │
Dog
```

Applications:

-   Knowledge Graphs
-   Semantic Networks
-   Ontologies

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Overall Structure

``` text
Knowledge
    │
    ├── Declarative
    ├── Procedural
    ├── Heuristic
    ├── Meta
    └── Structural
```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Software Engineering Perspective

Modern AI systems combine multiple knowledge types.

Example: AI Coding Assistant

-   Declarative → Programming syntax
-   Procedural → How to build an application
-   Heuristic → Best coding practices
-   Meta → Confidence score
-   Structural → Relationship between classes, components, and APIs

# MCQs

### Q1. Which type of knowledge represents facts?

A. Procedural

B. Heuristic

C. Declarative

D. Structural

**Answer:** C. Declarative

**Explanation:** Declarative Knowledge stores factual information.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q2. Which type explains how to perform a task?

A. Meta

B. Procedural

C. Declarative

D. Structural

**Answer:** B. Procedural

**Explanation:** Procedural Knowledge describes the steps required to
complete a task.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q3. Heuristic Knowledge is mainly based on:

A. Experience

B. Hardware

C. Compiler

D. Memory

**Answer:** A. Experience

**Explanation:** Heuristic Knowledge uses practical experience and
shortcuts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q4. Meta Knowledge is:

A. Knowledge about hardware

B. Knowledge about knowledge

C. Knowledge about software

D. Knowledge about databases

**Answer:** B. Knowledge about knowledge

**Explanation:** It describes the source, quality, and confidence of
existing knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q5. Structural Knowledge mainly describes:

A. Images

B. Relationships among concepts

C. Programs

D. Files

**Answer:** B. Relationships among concepts

**Explanation:** Structural Knowledge organizes concepts through
relationships.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q6. "Know What" refers to:

A. Declarative Knowledge

B. Procedural Knowledge

C. Meta Knowledge

D. Heuristic Knowledge

**Answer:** A. Declarative Knowledge

**Explanation:** Declarative Knowledge contains facts and concepts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q7. "Know How" refers to:

A. Declarative Knowledge

B. Structural Knowledge

C. Procedural Knowledge

D. Meta Knowledge

**Answer:** C. Procedural Knowledge

**Explanation:** It explains how to perform tasks.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q8. Which AI application commonly uses Heuristic Knowledge?

A. Chess AI

B. Calculator

C. Paint

D. Notepad

**Answer:** A. Chess AI

**Explanation:** Chess AI uses experience-based evaluation to choose
better moves.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q9. Knowledge Graphs mainly represent:

A. Procedural Knowledge

B. Structural Knowledge

C. Meta Knowledge

D. Heuristic Knowledge

**Answer:** B. Structural Knowledge

**Explanation:** Knowledge Graphs focus on relationships among entities.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Q10. Which statement is correct?

A. AI uses only one type of knowledge.

B. AI does not require Meta Knowledge.

C. AI combines multiple knowledge types for intelligent behaviour.

D. Structural Knowledge stores only images.

**Answer:** C. AI combines multiple knowledge types for intelligent
behaviour.

**Explanation:** Different knowledge types work together to improve AI
reasoning and decision making.

# One-Line Summary

Artificial Intelligence combines Declarative, Procedural, Heuristic,
