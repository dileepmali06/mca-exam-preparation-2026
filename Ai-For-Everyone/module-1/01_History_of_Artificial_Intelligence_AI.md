# 1. Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) did not suddenly begin with ChatGPT or
modern technology. The idea of creating intelligent machines has existed
for centuries.

The journey of AI started with a simple question:

> **"Can machines think like humans?"**

This question eventually became one of the biggest research areas in
Computer Science.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Evolution of Artificial Intelligence

## Ancient Greece

-   Philosophers imagined intelligent artificial beings.
-   No computers existed.
-   AI existed only as an idea.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1940s -- Birth of Digital Computers

-   Digital computers were developed.
-   Scientists realized machines could process information.
-   Research shifted from imagination to implementation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1950 -- Alan Turing

Alan Turing published **"Computing Machinery and Intelligence"** and
asked:

> **Can Machines Think?**

### Turing Test

The Turing Test evaluates whether a machine can behave intelligently
enough that a human cannot distinguish it from another human during
conversation.

**Definition**

> The Turing Test is a method proposed by Alan Turing to determine
> whether a machine can exhibit intelligent behavior indistinguishable
> from that of a human.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1956 -- Dartmouth Conference

The Dartmouth Conference is considered the official birth of Artificial
Intelligence.

Important points:

-   Organized at Dartmouth College.
-   John McCarthy coined the term **Artificial Intelligence**.
-   Researchers formally started AI as a scientific field.

### Important Exam Point

> John McCarthy is known as the **Father of Artificial Intelligence**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## AI Golden Period

After 1956:

-   Governments funded AI research.
-   Scientists believed human-level AI would arrive quickly.
-   Expectations were very high.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## AI Winter

AI Winter refers to the period when:

-   Research slowed down.
-   Funding decreased.
-   Public interest reduced.
-   Progress did not meet expectations.

### Definition

> AI Winter is a period during which AI research, funding, and public
> interest declined because expected progress was not achieved.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## AI Revival

AI became successful due to:

1.  Large amounts of data
2.  Powerful computers (CPU, GPU)
3.  Better Machine Learning algorithms

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1997 -- IBM Deep Blue

IBM's Deep Blue defeated World Chess Champion **Garry Kasparov**,
proving that AI could solve highly complex problems.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2012 -- Deep Learning Revolution

Deep Learning dramatically improved:

-   Image Recognition
-   Speech Recognition
-   Natural Language Processing
-   Computer Vision

Major companies began investing heavily in AI.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2022 -- Generative AI

ChatGPT introduced Generative AI to millions of users.

Modern AI can generate:

-   Text
-   Code
-   Images
-   Audio
-   Video

# Important Definitions

## Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence is the branch of computer science that develops
intelligent systems capable of performing tasks that normally require
human intelligence.

## Turing Test

The Turing Test is a method proposed by Alan Turing to determine whether
a machine behaves like a human during conversation.

## AI Winter

AI Winter is the period during which AI research and funding declined
due to limited progress.
