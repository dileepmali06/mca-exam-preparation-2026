# 6.2 Governance, Risk and Compliance Aspects in Cloud

## Introduction

Cloud adoption is continuously growing as organizations move their applications, services, and infrastructure to cloud platforms. While cloud computing offers scalability, flexibility, and cost savings, it also introduces challenges related to governance, risk management, and regulatory compliance.

To maximize the benefits of cloud computing while minimizing risks, organizations must adopt a Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) framework. A cloud governance framework helps automate security processes, improve visibility, ensure compliance, and establish best practices for cloud operations.

---

# Governance, Risk and Compliance (GRC)

GRC stands for:

* **Governance**
* **Risk Management**
* **Compliance**

These three components work together to ensure secure, efficient, and compliant cloud operations.

## Governance

Governance refers to the policies, procedures, standards, and controls used to manage cloud resources and services.

### Objectives of Governance

* Define organizational policies
* Manage cloud resources effectively
* Ensure accountability
* Improve security management
* Align cloud usage with business goals

---

## Risk Management

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, mitigating, and monitoring threats that may affect cloud resources.

### Examples of Cloud Risks

* Data breaches
* Account hijacking
* Malware attacks
* Data loss
* Service outages

### Objectives of Risk Management

* Identify threats
* Evaluate impact and probability
* Implement mitigation strategies
* Continuously monitor risks

---

## Compliance

Compliance refers to adhering to legal, regulatory, and industry standards related to security, privacy, and data protection.

### Examples

* GDPR
* HIPAA
* ISO 27001
* PCI-DSS

### Objectives of Compliance

* Protect customer data
* Meet legal requirements
* Avoid penalties and fines
* Improve trust and transparency

---

# Importance of Cloud Governance

Cloud governance helps organizations:

* Improve cloud security
* Reduce risks
* Ensure compliance
* Increase operational visibility
* Improve decision-making
* Establish accountability

A proper governance framework enables organizations to securely adopt and manage cloud technologies.

---

# 1. Improve Cloud Asset / Service Visibility

The first step in cloud governance is understanding all cloud assets and services being used within an organization.

## Asset Visibility

Cloud asset visibility refers to maintaining complete awareness of:

* Cloud applications
* Servers
* Databases
* Storage resources
* User accounts
* Business processes

Organizations must know what resources exist, who owns them, and how they are being used.

## Shadow IT

Shadow IT refers to cloud applications or services used without official approval from the IT department.

### Examples

* Personal Google Drive
* Dropbox
* Unauthorized SaaS applications

Shadow IT increases security risks because organizations lose visibility and control over sensitive data.

## Benefits

* Better security monitoring
* Improved compliance
* Reduced data leakage
* Enhanced operational control

---

# 2. Assess the Cloud Service Provider (CSP) Continuously

A Cloud Service Provider (CSP) is responsible for delivering cloud services and infrastructure.

### Examples

* Amazon Web Services (AWS)
* Microsoft Azure
* Google Cloud Platform (GCP)

Organizations must continuously evaluate cloud providers to ensure they meet security and compliance requirements.

## Evaluation Criteria

### Network Security

* Firewall protection
* Intrusion detection
* Secure communication

### Physical Security

* Secure data centers
* CCTV monitoring
* Biometric access control

### Compliance Certifications

* ISO 27001
* SOC Reports
* GDPR Compliance
* PCI-DSS

### Business Continuity

* Disaster recovery plans
* Backup policies
* High availability

### Data Recovery

* Data restoration procedures
* Recovery time objectives

Continuous assessment ensures cloud providers maintain required security standards.

---

# 3. Assign Business Ownership and Accountability for Critical Cloud Assets and Services

Every cloud asset and service should have a clearly defined owner.

## Cloud Asset Ownership

Examples of cloud assets:

* Applications
* Databases
* Storage buckets
* Virtual machines
* Security controls

Each asset should have:

* Defined ownership
* Assigned responsibilities
* Operational accountability

## Accountability

Accountability ensures that individuals or teams are responsible for:

* Security
* Maintenance
* Monitoring
* Compliance

## Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

Organizations should define SLAs with cloud providers covering:

* Availability
* Performance
* Backup policies
* Recovery objectives
* Security commitments

## Benefits

* Clear responsibilities
* Faster issue resolution
* Improved governance
* Better risk management

---

# 4. Know the Cloud Threat Landscape and Evaluate Risks

Organizations must understand the cloud threat landscape and continuously evaluate security risks.

## Cloud Threat Landscape

Common cloud threats include:

* Data breaches
* Malware attacks
* Insider threats
* Account hijacking
* Denial of Service (DoS) attacks
* Insecure APIs

Threats continuously evolve as technology advances.

## Risk Evaluation

Risk evaluation involves:

### Threat Identification

Identifying possible security threats.

### Risk Analysis

Determining:

* Probability of occurrence
* Potential impact

### Risk Mitigation

Implementing controls to reduce threats.

### Continuous Monitoring

Monitoring cloud resources for emerging risks.

## Benefits

* Reduced security incidents
* Improved preparedness
* Better protection of sensitive data

---

# 5. Leverage Standard Risk / Control Frameworks to Assess Compliance

Organizations should use industry-recognized frameworks to assess cloud security, compliance, and risk management.

## Purpose

These frameworks provide:

* Security guidelines
* Compliance requirements
* Risk assessment methodologies
* Best practices

---

## Cloud Security Alliance (CSA)

CSA provides cloud security best practices and governance frameworks.

### CSA GRC Stack

The GRC Stack helps organizations assess:

* Public clouds
* Private clouds
* Compliance requirements
* Security controls

### CSA Treacherous Twelve

A list of the top cloud security threats.

Examples:

* Data breaches
* Data loss
* Insecure APIs
* Account hijacking

---

## ISACA Framework

ISACA provides governance, audit, and cybersecurity frameworks used by enterprises worldwide.

---

## NIST Cybersecurity Framework

Developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST).

### Five Core Functions

1. Identify
2. Protect
3. Detect
4. Respond
5. Recover

The NIST framework helps organizations manage cybersecurity risks systematically.

---

## ISO 27017

An international standard focused specifically on cloud security controls.

### Objectives

* Improve cloud security
* Define cloud security responsibilities
* Protect cloud environments

---

## ISO 27018

An international standard focused on privacy protection in cloud environments.

### Objectives

* Protect personal information
* Improve privacy controls
* Support regulatory compliance

---

## CIS Controls

Developed by the Center for Internet Security (CIS).

### Focus Areas

* Access control
* Asset management
* Vulnerability management
* Monitoring and logging
* Security hardening

---

# Reducing IT Risk in Cloud Computing Governance

Cloud governance helps organizations reduce IT-related risks while managing cloud resources.

Organizations use cloud services in different ways:

### Temporary Computing Power

Using cloud infrastructure to meet short-term computing requirements.

### Software as a Service (SaaS)

Using applications hosted by cloud providers.

Examples:

* Google Workspace
* Microsoft 365
* Salesforce

### Platform as a Service (PaaS)

Using cloud platforms to build and deploy applications.

Examples:

* Google App Engine
* Azure App Service
* AWS Elastic Beanstalk

Different usage models introduce different governance and risk requirements.

# Cloud Computing Risk List

Organizations moving to the cloud must evaluate various risks.

## 1. Audit and Compliance Risks

Issues related to:

* Data jurisdiction
* Regulatory compliance
* Audit trails
* Access control

---

## 2. Security Risks

Threats affecting:

* Confidentiality
* Integrity
* Availability

Examples:

* Data breaches
* Malware
* Insider threats

---

## 3. Information Risks

Risks associated with:

* Intellectual property
* Sensitive business information
* Confidential documents

---

## 4. Performance and Availability Risks

Issues affecting:

* Application performance
* Service availability
* Uptime

Examples:

* Downtime
* Slow response times

---

## 5. Interoperability Risks

Challenges related to integration between:

* Different cloud services
* Multiple providers
* Hybrid cloud environments

---

## 6. Contract Risks

Risks caused by:

* Poorly understood agreements
* Hidden clauses
* Undefined responsibilities

---

## 7. Billing Risks

Financial risks associated with:

* Incorrect billing
* Resource overconsumption
* Cost overruns

Organizations should continuously monitor cloud spending to avoid unexpected costs.

Implementing Governance, Risk, and Compliance frameworks provides:

* Enhanced cloud security
* Improved visibility
* Better compliance management

* Improved trust and transparency

# Summary

Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) form the foundation of secure cloud adoption. Governance establishes policies and accountability, Risk Management identifies and mitigates threats, and Compliance ensures adherence to legal and regulatory requirements. Organizations should improve cloud visibility, continuously assess cloud providers, assign ownership, evaluate risks, adopt industry frameworks, and manage cloud-specific risks to ensure secure and efficient cloud operations.
